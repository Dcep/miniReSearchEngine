Add that to the rise cost of food , clothing , and travel , and almost every individual and family will feel the pinch . Fortunately , you can make your utility bill more manageable . Many step to reduce your bill apply to everyone , even those live in barrack . And even if your utility be currently pay a part of your rent , pay attention : By 2010 , only 10 percent of military personnel will live in government-owned residence , and many private or service-owned residence require resident to pay their own utility bill , report the EIA . 1 ) Turn down the heat . Much a we love a cozy home , lower the thermostat will instantly low your bill . The EIA say project that for every degree you low your heat , you can cut your heating cost by as much a 5 percent . At night , or when you be away , low the temperature a far a possible while protecting your health and the safety of your pipe . Bundle up with a throw on the sofa , get some warm slipper , and wear a sweater or fleece . If necessary , stay cozy at night with an electric blanket , which us less energy than heating the entire home or apartment . 2 ) Program the temperature . Make furnace setting automatic by installing a programmable thermostat . These device cost about $ 40 and be simple to install . 3 ) Replace light bulb . Replace traditional incandescent bulb with compact fluorescent light ( CFLs ) to save about 50 watt per light bulb and make a significant difference on utility bill . They do cost more to purchase , but they pay off over the life of the bulb . If you move frequently , you might want to keep the old bulb to leave behind in the fixture while take your investment in bulb with you . 4 ) Cut laundry cost . Wash clothe in cold water -- they will last longer and still get clean while saving energy . Hang clothe to dry , either outside or on a line in a garage , bathroom or spare room . If you do use the clothes dryer , try to use an Energy Star certify model , and be sure to clean the lint filter before every use to maximize air circulation . 5 ) Insulate . Carefully inspect your home for drafty spot where cold air can enter . The most common culprit be door and windows . Install weather strip and door sweep to block draft . Add old-fashioned `` draft dodger '' for a quick fix at exterior door . Other common area for air leak be lock , outlet , air condition unit and recess light fixture . Cover outside vent , include air condition unit . If possible , install insulate electrical outlet box and light fixture . The Energy Star program offer a free guide to home insulation at http : //www.energystar.gov/index.cfm ? c=diy.diy_index . 6 ) Use the sun . The sun be a great source of natural energy . Taking small step such a open the drape to let the sun 's warmth in during the day , and closing them -- or install insulate drape -- to keep heat in at night will make substantial difference in your heating bill . Leave light off if sunlight provide enough light by which to read , shower , cook or eat . 7 ) Choose budget bill . Consider contacting your utility provider to ask about bill a set amount each month . Called `` budget billing '' by some utility , the company average your annual utility bill and bill one-twelfth of the amount each month . Bills usually will be slightly low in the winter and summer and slightly high in the spring and fall . Most importantly , the bill will be a predictable amount each month to simplify budget plan . ( Those in Service-provided housing might already have this feature on their bill . ) If you find you ca n't pay your utility bill , get help . Many state offer program to help people keep the utility on when they have trouble pay the bill . Contact your utility company to learn about it plan , and be sure to mention that you be a military family . For instance , Ohio 's Patriot Plan be base on a law that state that utility ca n't be disconnect for nonpayment for any military reservist or National Guardsman deploy on active duty . In an emergency , organization such a the Salvation Army might be able to help . These action share one common feature : The No . 1 action you can take to rein in your utility bill be to pay attention to the energy you use , and try to use only what you need . When you use utility carefully , you 'll be able to reduce your bill , while still enjoy the comfort of your home , while you 're in the Service and beyond .
