Smart meters give real-time information on a homes energy use and cost. Under EU rules, they will be rolled out to most households in Britain over the next six years. Families will face higher bills to cover the introduction of the devices but there is no upfront charge. Families will face higher bills to cover the introduction of smart meters However, power firm First Utility is sending out emails to customers telling them they will get a smart meter for free when they sign up to one of its fixed-rate deals. IS IT TIME TO SWITCH? Energy price tariffs are in a constant merry-go-round with suppliers constantly battling to pinch customers - you - from each other. Shrewd consumers can take advantage of this by doing exactly that - moving deals every six months to a year to ensure they are on THE cheapest deal. Even moving every other year will save you significant amounts. Suppliers offer their cheapest rates via online tariffs so if you're ready to switch, it will certainly pay to do so. If you are one of the millions of people who have NEVER switched (i.e. stuck with your original supplier), then you will DEFINITELY save a big chunk of cash, possibly as much as 300 a year. Prices are different all over the country and the cheapest supplier for you will depend where you live. You only need to be interested in the tariff that is going to be cheapest where you live, so do your own comparison to find the best price.
