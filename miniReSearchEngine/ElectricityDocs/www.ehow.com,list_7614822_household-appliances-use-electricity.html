What Household Appliances Use the Most Electricity?

By Lee Morgan, eHow Contributor
  •  Pin
  •  Share
  •  Tweet
  •  Stumble
  •  Share
Found This Helpful

The next time you get your electric bill in the mail, take a good look at the dollar amount you owe and know that 20 percent of the cost is generated by your household appliances. While heating and air conditioning can be expensive, and televisions and computers can eat up their fair share of juice, a few common home appliances gobble up energy in big amounts that cost you a considerable amount each month. Knowing which ones use the most might help you scale back the use of these pricey devices when possible.

Refrigerator

Unfortunately you can’t just turn off the refrigerator. or else all the food would spoil. Regardless, the refrigerator is the appliance that uses the most energy in the typical home. While some appliances may use more energy in a shorter amount of time, the refrigerator is one of the few things that must operate 24 hours a day to serve its purpose.

With approximately a 750 watt requirement being common on a 16 cubic foot frost-free refrigerator, according to the Energy Savers website, the constant use of this appliance makes is an expensive thing to have. The average household will use more kilowatt-hours in a year than any other single electrical appliance, and an average household will spend nearly $100 just to have a refrigerator each year, according to the Save on Utilities website.

Washer and Dryer

The laundry isn’t done at home in every house, but many people have both a washer and a dryer at home. If you do, then you can guarantee they are among the appliances that use the most energy. If you combined the energy usage of the washer and dryer it would far surpass the usage of the refrigerator, but divided they are only a close second and third.

The amount of energy needed to run a washer and dryer is among the highest of all the household appliances, but fortunately they only run on occasion. Still, the washer and dryer is likely to use nearly 2,000 kilowatt-hours per year and cost the average family more than $150 in electric bills, according to the Save on Utilities website.

Range/Oven

Depending on how much you cook at home, the range and oven may or may not be on the upper end of the list. However, someone who cooks for a family regularly on an electric appliance can be certain the oven and range together are one of the biggest energy hogs in the home. The average American household spends around $70 per year on electric cooking, not including the microwave, adding around 700 kilowatt hours to the home’s energy consumption.
