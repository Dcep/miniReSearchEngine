Energy firms woo customers with promise of a free smart meter - which they shouldn't have to pay for anyway

By Money Mail

Published: 11:51 EST, 5 March 2014 | Updated: 20:45 EST, 5 March 2014

26

View
comments

Energy firms are wooing customers by promising to give them smart meters for free — even though they are supposed to do so anyway.

Smart meters give real-time information on a home’s energy use and cost. Under EU rules, they will be rolled out to most households in Britain over the next six years.

Families will face higher bills to cover the introduction of the devices — but there is no upfront charge.

Families will face higher bills to cover the introduction of smart meters

However, power firm First Utility is sending out emails to customers telling them they will get a smart meter for free when they sign up to one of its fixed-rate deals.

A spokesman says the email is to let its customers know they can get Smart Meters faster than with other firms, because it is at the forefront of rolling out the technology.

IS IT TIME TO SWITCH?

Energy price tariffs are in a constant merry-go-round with suppliers constantly battling to pinch customers - you - from each other.

Shrewd consumers can take advantage of this by doing exactly that - moving deals every six months to a year to ensure they are on THE cheapest deal. Even moving every other year will save you significant amounts.

Suppliers offer their cheapest rates via online tariffs so if you're ready to switch, it will certainly pay to do so.

If you are one of the millions of people who have NEVER switched (i.e. stuck with your original supplier), then you will DEFINITELY save a big chunk of cash, possibly as much as £300 a year.

Prices are different all over the country and the cheapest supplier for you will depend where you live. You only need to be interested in the tariff that is going to be cheapest where you live, so do your own comparison to find the best price.
